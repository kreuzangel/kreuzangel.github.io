    Research

    Earth's geoid taken from GFZ

    My research experience deal with the spatial and spectral analysis of gravity changes recorded by the Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment (GRACE) and its Follow-on mission (GRACE-FO). The analysis of GRACE/GRACE-FO data allow to infer Earth's mass changes on a broad spatial scale (circa.350 km). Most research efforts working with this data set focus on the separation of gravity signal into different mass change sources. One of the most complex one, deal with the deformation response of the Earth due to large surface mass changes known as Glacial Isostatic Adjustment (GIA).

    During the consecution of my master thesis researching at the Alfred Wegener Institut, I delved into the analysis of numerical simulations of global glacier mass changes and their imprinted GIA signal since the Little Ice Age.

    The research findings highlight the need to include present-day mass changes and lateral variations of Earth’s rheology within GIA models to improve the accuracy of ice mass balance assessments derived from GRACE/GRACE-FO data.
